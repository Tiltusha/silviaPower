ENGINE SPECIFICATION

The Nissan SR20DET is a 2.0 liter (1998 cc) inline four-cylinder petrol internal combustion engine that is part of the SR family. Serially produced from 1989 to 2002. It was a turbocharged version of the SR20DE and a replacement for the CA18DET. The SR20DET, like the CA18DET, was equipped with a turbocharger and intercooler. It was a popular four-cylinder engine found in various Nissan vehicles, most notably the Nissan Silvia and 180SX.

nissan silvia s13

In 1988, the new S13 Silvia was released in Japan, and it became the most popular of the Silvia models. In Japan and most other countries, the first S13 Silvia was equipped with the CA18DE and CA18DET engines. The Silvia K's was a high-performance turbocharged model that produced 175 hp.

nissan silvia s14

The Silvia S14 was launched on the Japanese market in 1993, and, following the S13, was very popular among buyers. The S14 had almost everything from the S13, except for a new, more rounded body instead of the angular S13. The SR engine undergoes minor modifications. Also on the new S14 there are now new hubs with five studs. This was followed by modifications to the body, giving a more aggressive look to the front end.

nissan silvia s15

In 1999, Nissan introduced the new Silvia S15 to the Japanese market. The S15 was still equipped with the same SR20, but now the top-end S15 Spec R version produced 250 hp and came with a six-speed transmission. There was also a special version of the non-turbo Spec S Autech, with a power of 200 hp. Production of the S15 ceased in 2002, and the history of Silvia stops there for now.
